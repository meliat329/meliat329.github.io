Student Health Leaders Redesign

Date: Fall and Winter 2020/2021

Role: Group Member

Course: Service Design I and II

Skills: Service Design, User Research, Mural

Redesigning the Student Health Leaders program was a part of my two-quarter capstone course led by Amy O'Keefe for the Segal Design Certificate. The Student Health Leaders Program is four half-day workshops that help Chicago Public School students become empowered health advocates within their school and communities. This class was split up into two parts: research during the first quarter, and prototyping in the second.

Insights and Synthesis

Researched the overall program needs by interviewing students who went through the workshops and health care workers who spoke at the workshops and synthesized our insights.

Service Design Tools

Used our insights to employ various service design tools such as empathy maps, service blueprints, journey maps, and experience maps.

Opportunity Areas

Identified major opportunity areas and brainstormed service directions for each opportunity area.

Post-Program Experience

Assigned to prototyping and redesigning the post-program experience.

Brainstorming

Used our various insights about the post-program experience (or lack of) to brainstorm ways to meet the participants and clients wants and needs. Placed these ideas on an "Impact vs Feasibility" 2X2.

Prototyping

Prototyped three main service ideas that ranked highest on our 2X2. These included a project implementation club, a general school club, and a mentorship program.

Testing

Tested our prototypes with previous SHL students and the client. We found that our mentorship idea was liked most by both the students and the client.

Design. Build. Test. Repeat.

Used iterative design to refine our mentorship program, and named it "MentorSHLip."

Final Program

With our time in the class coming to an end, we created a final presentation to present to the client to receive feedback. While we had made a lot of progress in appealing to our users, the students, the client found that with the way it was currently it would not be feasible for their busy schedules. With more time we would have worked to address this issue.
