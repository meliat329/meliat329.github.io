Legato Application

Date: Spring 2021

Role: Lead Group Member

Course: Human-Computer Interaction Studio

Skills: UI/UX Design, User Research, Figma, Human-Computer Interaction

Needfinding

My group and I began by interviewing people interested in activities we knew next to nothing about to see if there were opportunity areas for an app. I interviewed someone who is a musical performance major and found that the classical music listening experience is not as good as it could be as most music listening apps cater to popular music and use the song-album-artist method of categorization, which does not make sense with classical music.

Potential User Research

My group and I then interviewed people that would be potential users of the app. This included people who listen to classical music for enjoyment, people who study classical music, and professors who use it to teach.This was to gain a better understanding of our problem area as none of us are too familiar with classical music.

Competitor Research

We then researched our competitors such as Spotify, YouTube, and Idagio. None of these seemed to completely cover what classical music listeners need.

Design. Build. Test. Repeat.

We started with low fidelity prototyping on paper. We honed in on finding a good search method for classical music, and identified four main ways someone may want to search for classical music: performer, composer, title, and conductor. We also figured users may want to combine these methods. We tested each of our prototypes and refined our design each time.

Final Prototype

Our final prototype was a wireframe on Figma that used simple buttons to select which way you would like to search. We have a separate playlist tab because combining playlist with some of our search options would not make sense. Finally our name Legato was chosen because legato means to play connected and smoothly, which is exactly how our app experience should be.
